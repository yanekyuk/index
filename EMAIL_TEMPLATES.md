Let's advance it further.

There will be three types of emails:

1. Connection Request Emails: Real-time, triggered when someone wants to connect with you.
2. Mutual Opt-In Emails (Double Opt-In Intros): Sent when both people approve a connection
3. Weekly Inbox Discovery Emails: Sent conditionally, only when new high-relevancy people appear

# Real Time Emails

## Template

Subject

> ✨ Alice wants to connect with you 

Body

> Hey Bob, 
> You’ve got a new connection request on Index, **Alice** wants to connect with you. 
>
> 👉 **Go to Index to approve** 
>
> What could happen between you two:
> You’re refining how agents negotiate access in private spaces, and Alice has been working through similar coordination problems from a systems angle. Your current focus meets her recent exploration - it’s the kind of overlap where one conversation can save weeks of circling alone. 
>
> If you’re curious, I’ll make the connection. If not, everything stays quiet. 
>
> —Index

## Rules

- Only send when a real connection request is made.
- No follow-ups if the user ignores it.
- No information is shared with the requester unless X approves.

>
> —Your discovery agent, quietly cheering from the background

# Weekly Inbox Discovery Emails

## Frequency Logic

Weekly email sends **only if the two following are true**:

- There are **1 or more new high-relevance matches**,
- The matches are meaningfully aligned with the user’s current direction.

## Template

Subject

> People who are closest to what you’re working on right now

Body

> Hey Seren,
> Index saw a few people this week whose work lines up unusually well with the things you’re trying to move forward — fundraising, agent deployments, protocol scaling, semantic web thinking, and the early versions of a discovery network.
>
> 👉 **Open your Inbox**
>
> **Closest to your direction this week:**
>
> **1. Alice — early-stage crypto investor + protocol tooling****
> **You’re raising a pre-seed round to fund development and onboarding, and Alice has been backing infra teams working on open coordination layers. She’s been exploring “semantic interfaces” for networks — very close to what you’re looking for in investor conversations.
>
> **2. Bob — builds internal AI copilots for enterprise orgs****
> **You’re validating match quality and privacy inside real communication channels. Bob has deployed AI agents across Slack and Teams and already solved many of the rollout and UX challenges you’re about to face.
>
> **3. Carla — researcher building agent interoperability tooling****
> **You’re working toward powering matching across ChatGPT, Claude, and Google Agent Space. Carla has been experimenting with multi-model agent protocols — almost exactly the direction you’re pushing.
>
> **4. Daniel — tokenomics engineer for staking & curation markets****
> **You’re designing a market where agents stake to propose high-relevancy matches. Daniel has built similar staking schemas and run simulations around adversarial behavior — shortcuts you probably want.
>
> **5. Elena — founder coordinating 3K+ user communities****
> **You’re seeding a 5,000-person, high-signal graph. Elena runs communities where these users already gather. Her ecosystems could accelerate your network effects.
>
> **6. Farid — senior engineer building Slack/Discord integrated apps****
> **You’re integrating agents into Slack, Discord, and web surfaces. Farid has built production add-ons for both platforms and already solved the hardest permissioning and background-task issues.
>
> **7. Gia — investor deeply curious about semantic search + AI infra****
> **You’re looking for investors that actually get the semantic web foundation. Gia has been researching schema-free discovery systems — rare alignment for investor calls.

> **8. Henry — protocol researcher focused on privacy-preserving matching****
> **You’re building a private discovery protocol where people speak in natural language. Henry has prototyped ways to match without exposing raw messages — extremely close to your privacy model.
>
> If anyone feels close to where you’re heading, I can connect you.
>  If not, they’ll stay in your Inbox until it’s the right moment.
>
> 👉 **Open your Inbox**
>
> —Index, keeping track of the people thinking like you

# Frequency and Behavioral Rules Summary

| Email Type                         | Sending Rule                                                 | Notes                                                        |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Real-Time (Connection Request)** | Send immediately. Never batch.                               | Only send to the receiving side.                             |
| **Double Opt-In (Intro Email)**    | Send only when both accept.                                  | One email sent to both people. Always warm, minimal, “take it from here”. |
| **Weekly Inbox Email**             | Send only if 3+ new aligned people surfaced, **OR** the system determines the week had unusual relevance (e.g., major fundraising, hiring, collaboration signals). | If nothing meaningful happened → no email.                   |
